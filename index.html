ABOUT US

History

Trapholt, established in 1988, stands as a cultural gem in Kolding, Denmark, showcasing a rich tapestry of Danish arts and crafts. The museum's very name, a fusion of "Tr√¶" (wood) and "holt" (grove), underscores its unique focus on Danish artistic traditions. Designed by the renowned architect Hans Hollein, the iconic building graces the landscape overlooking Kolding Fjord, making it a visual masterpiece in itself. Trapholt's commitment to preserving and promoting Danish design heritage is evident through its extensive collections that span across modern art and design. The museum curates diverse exhibitions, featuring both Danish and international artists, creating a dynamic space for artistic expression and cultural exchange. This emphasis on inclusivity and global perspectives adds depth to the museum's offerings, making it a dynamic hub for art enthusiasts and cultural explorers alike. Education is a cornerstone of Trapholt's mission, and the museum actively engages visitors of all ages. Through educational programs, workshops, and interactive exhibits, Trapholt aims to foster a deeper understanding and appreciation for Danish arts and crafts. This dedication to education not only enhances the visitor experience but also contributes to the cultural enrichment of the community. The museum's surroundings are as captivating as its contents, with scenic gardens enveloping the iconic building. These green spaces add an extra layer to the museum experience, providing a tranquil environment for contemplation and relaxation. Trapholt, therefore, functions not only as a repository of art but also as a cultural oasis, inviting visitors to immerse themselves in the beauty of both the artistic creations within its walls and the natural landscapes that embrace it. In essence, Trapholt is more than just a museum; it is a dynamic and evolving cultural institution that plays a vital role in shaping Denmark's artistic landscape. By seamlessly blending tradition with innovation, Trapholt contributes to the ongoing dialogue surrounding Danish arts and crafts, ensuring that the country's rich design heritage continues to thrive and inspire generations to come.

Mission

Danish design is known as combination of designs from over the world, we want to make our visitors part of our community - because we know that their culture is implemented in our design. Our motto "When all cultures meet..." represents this mindset emphasized by logo inspired by Nanna's Ditzel "Bench for Two" created in order to integrate. Main colors reflect our values - red (love - connecting people), orange (change - breaking rules in Danish design), pink (playfulness - playing with shapes and colors). Our aim is to make Trapholt extraordinary place where everyone could experience museum on new level - by fully diving into art and becoming anew creators - cause the past is now, but future is what outstanding we are creating currently.
